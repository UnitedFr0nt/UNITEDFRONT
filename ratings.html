Ratings for movies:
1/5
If your movie gets this rating, it's not good. You should not strive to make a movie like this unless you're shitposting.
1.5/5
If you get a 1.5, your movie still sucks. We just decided to add a .5 for additional effort.
2/5
If you get a 2, your movie is better than a movie that got a 1/5. It's bad, but it isn't garbage.
2.5/5
If you get a 2.5, your movie is better than a movie that got a 1.5/5. It's bad, but it isn't garbage (.5 for additional effort made).
3/5
The median. Most newbie movies will get this rating. You're doing better, but it's not exactly the best.
3.5/5
If you get this rating, it's still pretty okay. You did put in some additional effort, which means you get an additional .5 to the rating.
4/5
This rating you get if your movie is great. It's better than a 3/5 and just overall should be looked at for inspiration.
4.5/5
If you get a 4.5/5, you're nearly at the point for a perfect movie. You've put in a lot of effort and we here at the United Front appreciate you not submitting garbage.
5/5
The perfect movie. Any movies that's amazing/inspirational should get this rating.
